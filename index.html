Active Attention via Bayesian Exploration (Github) (PDF link)
Research Course Project
Attention based models are widely used across different domains in machine learning. To the best of our knowledge, modern attention mechanisms don't explicitly reward surprise, despite the expectation that these mechanisms already seek out surprise. This paper applies curiosity-based exploration strategies to an attention model. These strategies involve augmenting the reward with an information gain term encouraging the agent to seek out areas it will deem surprising. Two approaches are taken to compute an information gain term: applying stochastic regularization techniques (SRT), and applying Variational Information Maximizing Exploration. The SRT-based experiments indicated that augmenting the reward with curiosity resulted in both higher test accuracy and less epochs to train, compared to the SRT-based baseline. Furthermore, we found that adding uncertainty to the model via SRTs can preserve accuracy without compromising computational cost.
